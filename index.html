timestamp
Exchange or feed time for this snapshot (ISO format). All rows in one snapshot share the same timestamp.
Side
Whether the row is an Ask (sell interest) or Bid (buy interest) at that futures price level.
future_strike
ES futures price level for this order-book row (the ladder price you see in the simulator).
MBO
Market-by-order depth: a list of sizes at this level. The app sums these values to show total quantity at the price.
MBO_pulling_stacking
Flow metric describing whether liquidity is being added (stacking) or removed (pulling) at the level; useful for order-flow analysis.
current_es_price
Underlying ES futures reference price from the feed (stored in ticks in the CSV; the server converts to dollars for display).
spx_strike
SPX strike used when computing option sensitivities for this futures level (aligned strike in the equity index options chain).
t
Time to expiration in years for the options used in the Greek calculations (year fraction).
spx_price
Spot level of the S&P 500 index at this snapshot, used as the underlying for the option model.
call_delta … call_vega
First-order sensitivities of the call option price: delta (underlying), gamma (curvature), theta (time decay per day), vega (implied-vol), rho (rates), plus higher-order terms below.
call_charm
Sensitivity of delta to the passage of time (also called delta decay).
call_rho
Sensitivity of option value to interest rates.
call_vanna
Sensitivity of delta to implied volatility (cross derivative of spot and vol).
call_vomma
Sensitivity of vega to implied volatility (vol-of-vol).
put_charm … put_vomma
Same Greek families for the put at the matching SPX strike. Put delta is negative; other signs follow the model.

Order book: four bars under each δ are Δ, Γ, Θ, V (call on asks, put on bids). Heights use min–max scaling across the 20 visible rows per Greek—so you see relative change across the ladder, not raw size (adjacent strikes are often numerically close). Hover a single bar for that Greek’s name and value. If a Greek is almost flat across those rows, bars stay similar—that reflects the data.
